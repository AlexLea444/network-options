Network Options for Apartment

1. Powerline Ethernet Adapters

Plug a Powerline Ethernet Adapter into an outlet by the router, connect via ethernet cable, then plug another Powerline Ethernet Adapter next to your/my computers, and connect those to the computers via ethernet cable.

Pros

  • Can get 3 adapters for ~$100.
  • High chance of "working".

Cons

  • Reliability is not so good, and dependent on circuits.
    • Could be used in tandem with #2.

2. Ethernet Cable Runners

The most direct solution, just run the necessary cables to get ethernet to our rooms. There are cable runners and other tools that can be used to hide them. I could come up with some designs and run them by you.

Pros

  • Most stable connection
  • Reasonable pricing (probably <$100)

Cons

  • Most difficult set-up
  • Most intrusive/potentially ugly

3. MoCA -- Network over Coax

Use Coax (the round wall connectors) to do the same thing as in #1, but MoCA is much more reliable and high-speed, almost to the level of pure Ethernet.

Pros

  • Reliable and high-speed
  • Physically unintrusive

Cons

  • Most expensive option, on scale of $200 for both of us or $120 for one
  • Security/feasibility depends on how the apartment has it set up
    • Conflicting reports on MoCA for Apartments

Other Notes

We don't have to choose exclusively one of the three. For example, with any option we can first look into moving the router into your room, and then #1 and #3 would require only one pair of adapters. Also, I can just suck it up.
